Augmented Reality Sandbox

Developed a game for kids, based on the UC Davis Sandbox for an event at work. The objective of the game was to navigate the fishes to the destination, using gestures/thermocol blocks(any obstacle to the Kinect!). The custom setup and precision of detecting obstacles were challenging, not to mention the callibration of Kinect. I contributed to the game dev, collision detection and flocking algorithm. The possibilites of a depth image are tremendous, imagine if our smartphones could capture the 3rd dimension…

  • Software/Technologies: C++, OpenCV, Kinect, OpenFrameworks

SteerQuest

We were a team of 6 for this hackathon conducted as a part of ABAI Fest, hosted as a day-long challenge on HackerEarth. We developed a simple flocking algorithm, based on OpenSteer. The problem involved increasing level of complexities to take a flock of sheep from source to the destination (barn). We built a visualizer in Blender which recived data from the solver over socket communication. Hosting and managing the event all the technical challenges(including wi-fi) was fun. I helped develop the bridge between Blender and C++ solver using Socket API and tweaked the solver for creating dynamic obstacles for a higher level.

  • Don't Miss this: SteerQuest
  • Date: 2016
  • Software/Technologies: C++, Python, Blender Game Engine

Machine Learning

I belive my interest for Data Science never will stop. Worked on quite a few projects both in Undergrad and Masters. Detecting Network intrusions and reducing false alarm was my first ML endeavour. Our paper was published by Elsevier in the journal of ICECIT 2012. We also did Speech recognition, and I was exploring feature extraction techniques and libraries. During my Masters, I continued this interest to identify Musical Instruments from a song, the idea was similar but processing speech and music had to be different. Pyhton was the best in terms of various ML and audio processing library support. Next was a desire to tinker with OpenCV and best way was to make something, and I built a Handwriting recognition tool, which needed some training samples from the user. I used Octave and C++ for this project.

2DTO3D

This project is one of my Dream projects and is just a bare bone building block as of now. It tries to take a baby step in solving the most complex probelm of depth estimation from a Single image. I attempted to build primitive modeling as a first step, so that a user can interactively model parts from an image. The most important assumption or aid here is Humans can perceive depth faster in comparison to solving a 4 by 4 View Matrix, so why not use it.

  • Date: 2015
  • Software/Technologies: C++, Model Reconstruction, OpenCV, OpenGL
